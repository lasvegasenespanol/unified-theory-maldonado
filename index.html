Executive Summary

We model our 4-D universe as a brane in a higher-D bulk. The effective FRW dynamics add a high-energy correction ($\rho^2$) and a dark-radiation term ($\mathcal C/a^4$). A single parameter—the brane tension $\lambda$—must simultaneously fit (i) the SGWB spectral break $f_{\rm br}(\lambda) \propto \lambda^{1/4}$ and (ii) the early-radiation excess $\Delta N_{\rm eff}$ via $\mathcal C$. This gives a falsifiable link across PTA (now) and LISA (near-future).

Grand Equation & Test Relations

$$H^2 \,=\, \frac{8\pi G}{3}\,\rho\!\left(1+\frac{\rho}{2\lambda}\right) \, + \, \frac{\Lambda_{4}}{3} \, + \, \frac{\mathcal{C}}{a^{4}} \qquad (k=0)$$
