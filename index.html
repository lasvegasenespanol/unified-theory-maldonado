Executive summary

We model our 4‑D universe as a brane embedded in a higher‑D bulk. In flat FRW the effective expansion law acquires a high‑energy ρ2 piece (controlled by the brane tension λ) and a dark‑radiation term C/a4. Two clean tests follow:

  • GW spectral break: a broken‑power‑law SGWB with break frequency \( f_{\rm br}(\lambda) \propto \lambda^{1/4} \).
  • Radiation excess: the dark‑radiation constant maps to \( \Delta N_{\rm eff} \) via \( C/\rho_{\gamma,0} = (7/8)(4/11)^{4/3} \, \Delta N_{\rm eff} \).

Falsifiability: the same λ must satisfy both sectors. If it fails either, the model is wrong.

Key equations

Modified Friedmann (flat, k=0)
$$ H^2 = \frac{8\pi G}{3}\,\rho\Bigl(1 + \frac{\rho}{2\lambda}\Bigr) + \frac{\Lambda_4}{3} + \frac{C}{a^4} $$

$$ f_{\rm br}(\lambda) = \alpha\,\lambda^{1/4}, \qquad \frac{C}{\rho_{\gamma,0}} = \frac{7}{8}\Bigl(\frac{4}{11}\Bigr)^{4/3}\,\Delta N_{\rm eff} $$
