Executive Summary

We model our 4‑D universe as a brane embedded in a higher‑D bulk. Projecting the higher‑D field equations yields a modified 4‑D Friedmann equation with a high‑energy correction and a dark‑radiation term. A single parameter—the brane tension $\lambda$—controls two clean, near‑term tests: a break in the stochastic gravitational‑wave background (SGWB) and an early‑time radiation excess $\Delta N_\mathrm{eff}$. The same $\lambda$ must satisfy both sectors, making the framework falsifiable.
