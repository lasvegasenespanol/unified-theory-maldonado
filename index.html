Plain-English Summary

This project proposes a testable brane–world cosmology. Our 4‑D universe (a “brane”) sits in a higher‑D bulk. The resulting 4‑D expansion law gets a high‑energy correction and a “dark‑radiation” term. A single parameter—the brane tension $\lambda$— must simultaneously match a gravitational‑wave spectral break and the CMB/BBN radiation budget. That gives a clean pass/fail test.

The Core Equation

$$ H^2 \,=\, \frac{8\pi G}{3}\,\rho\!\left(1+\frac{\rho}{2\lambda}\right) \, + \, \frac{\Lambda_{4}}{3} \, + \, \frac{\mathcal{C}}{a^{4}} \quad (k=0) $$

Test Link #1: GW Spectral Break

$$f_{\rm br}(\lambda)\;\propto\;\lambda^{1/4}$$ Transition from the $\rho^2$ era to standard 4‑D expansion— checkable with Pulsar Timing Arrays now and LISA next.

Test Link #2: Early‑Radiation Budget

$$\frac{\mathcal{C}}{\rho_{\gamma,0}}\;=\;\frac{7}{8}\left(\frac{4}{11}\right)^{4/3}\Delta N_{\rm eff}$$ A single $\lambda$ must fit both the break and $\Delta N_{\rm eff}$.

What Would Confirm or Falsify It?

  • Confirm: A GW break at the predicted frequency from the same $\lambda$ that also satisfies CMB/BBN $\Delta N_{\rm eff}$.
  • Falsify: No break where required, or the necessary $\lambda$ violates $\Delta N_{\rm eff}$ bounds.
