Executive Summary

We model our 4‑D universe as a brane embedded in a higher‑D bulk. The effective 4‑D dynamics on the brane yield a modified Friedmann law with a high‑energy term and a dark‑radiation term. A single parameter — the brane tension $\lambda$ — controls two independent observables:

  • a break frequency in the stochastic GW background: $f_{\rm br}(\lambda) \propto \lambda^{1/4}$, and
  • an extra radiation component mapped via $\displaystyle \frac{\mathcal C}{\rho_{\gamma,0}} = \frac{7}{8}\left(\frac{4}{11}\right)^{4/3}\Delta N_{\rm eff}$.

Falsifiable rule: the same $\lambda$ must fit both the GW break and $\Delta N_{\rm eff}$ constraints. If it fails either, the model is wrong.

Grand Equations

Modified Friedmann (flat FRW, $k=0$):

$$ H^2 \,=\, \frac{8\pi G}{3}\,\rho\!\left(1+\frac{\rho}{2\lambda}\right)\; +\; \frac{\Lambda_4}{3}\; +\; \frac{\mathcal C}{a^4} $$

Break–tension map:

$$ f_{\rm br}(\lambda) \;\propto\; \lambda^{1/4} $$

Dark radiation–$\Delta N_{\rm eff}$:

$$ \frac{\mathcal C}{\rho_{\gamma,0}}\;=\;\frac{7}{8}\left(\frac{4}{11}\right)^{4/3}\,\Delta N_{\rm eff} $$
