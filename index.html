The Big Idea

The model unifies gravity, electromagnetism, and the strong & weak nuclear forces by embedding them in a higher-dimensional String/M-theory geometry. The Big Bang is reinterpreted as a brane supernova (a higher-dimensional energy event) whose aftershock seeded our 4D universe.

Key Equation

H^2 = (8πG/3) ρ (1 + ρ / 2λ) + 𝒞/a^4 − k/a^2 + Λ₄/3

A modified Friedmann equation with a high-energy ρ² correction (brane tension λ), a dark-radiation term 𝒞/a⁴ from bulk effects, curvature k, and an effective 4D cosmological constant Λ₄.

Why It Matters

  • Unifies all four forces in one framework
  • Replaces the singular Big Bang with a physical higher-dimensional cause
  • Makes testable predictions: gravitational-wave spectrum features, CMB imprints, and dark radiation
  • Addresses hierarchy problem, black holes, and early-universe expansion

Testable Predictions

  • Broken power-law stochastic gravitational-wave background (LISA, PTAs)
  • Specific CMB anisotropy/non-Gaussian signatures (CMB-S4, LiteBIRD)
  • Effective ΔN_eff & dark-radiation constraints (CMB+BAO)
  • Warped-dimension effects near unification scales (collider/precision tests)
