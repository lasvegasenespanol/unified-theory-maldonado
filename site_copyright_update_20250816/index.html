Abstract

We model our 4‑D universe as a brane embedded in a higher‑D bulk. Projecting the higher‑D field equations yields a modified 4‑D Friedmann equation with a high‑energy correction and a dark‑radiation term. The framework is falsifiable because a single parameter (the brane tension $\lambda$) must simultaneously match a stochastic GW break frequency $f_\mathrm{{br}}(\lambda)\propto\lambda^{{1/4}}$ and an early‑time radiation excess $\Delta N_\mathrm{{eff}}$ via a fixed mapping.

$$ H^2 \,=\, \frac{{8\pi G}}{{3}}\,\rho\!\left(1+\frac{{\rho}}{{2\lambda}}\right) \, + \, \frac{\Lambda_4}{3} \, + \, \frac{\mathcal{{C}}}{a^{4}}\,. $$
