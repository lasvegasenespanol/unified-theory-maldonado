Data & Repro Pack for “A testable brane‑world unification: one parameter links a GW spectral break and ΔN_eff” (Maldonado, 2025-08-14)

Summary
-------
Machine‑readable files and a minimal script sufficient to regenerate the figures and key checks:
(1) a broken‑power‑law fit to a pulsar‑timing‑array (PTA) stochastic GW spectrum with a single break frequency f_br,
(2) LISA sensitivity overlays in CSV (ESA‑style headers),
(3) a compact two‑page PDF (equations + plots), and
(4) a small JSON of best‑fit parameters (demo).

Column schema
-------------
LISA CSVs: f_Hz [Hz], Sn_1_per_Hz [1/Hz], label [string]
PTA CSV:  freq_Hz [Hz], log10rho_median, log10rho_p16, log10rho_p84

How to reproduce
----------------
1) Run reproduce_posteriors.py (Python 3 with numpy, pandas, matplotlib).
2) It reads the PTA CSV, fits a broken power law in log‑space, prints f_br, and saves plots + JSON.
3) To go from demo to publication‑grade: replace PTA_public_anchor_demo.csv with an official export (e.g., exported_pta_spectrum.csv derived from the NANOGrav KDE free‑spectrum package) and use an official LISA sensitivity CSV.

Purpose
-------
To enable independent inspection and quick regeneration of:
- The SGWB broken‑power‑law shape and break frequency f_br(λ) ∝ λ^(1/4).
- The mapping of the dark‑radiation constant to ΔN_eff via
  C / ρ_γ,0 = (7/8) * (4/11)^(4/3) * ΔN_eff.
